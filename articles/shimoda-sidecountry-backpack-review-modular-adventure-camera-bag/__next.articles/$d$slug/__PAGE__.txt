3:Tda2,<p>The Shimoda Sidecountry is a lightweight, modular backpack system designed for adventure photographers who need quick camera access without sacrificing hiking comfort. With a detachable side-loading top loader, roll-top expansion, and a carrying system built for real terrain, it bridges the gap between camera bag and outdoor pack.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Key Specs</h2>
<ul>
<li>Sizes: 28L and 32L</li>
<li>Top loader sizes: Small, Medium, Large (Medium included)</li>
<li>Laptop: Up to 16&quot;</li>
<li>Material: Recycled Cordura nylon, PFAS-free</li>
<li>Aluminum frame with breathable air-mesh back panel</li>
<li>Vegan-friendly construction</li>
<li>Hidden AirTag sleeve</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Design &amp; Modularity</h2>
<p>The standout feature is the side-loading top loader — Shimoda&#039;s first design built exclusively around side access. Swing the bag under your arm, unzip, and grab your camera without setting the pack down. The top loader detaches entirely and converts to a standalone shoulder bag or waist pack for lighter excursions.</p>
<p>The roll-top adds capacity for a drone, GoPro, helmet, or extra layers. Inside, bright white interiors make it easy to spot small items like batteries and memory cards at a glance.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Comfort &amp; Carrying</h2>
<img src="/media/articles/shimoda-sidecountry-backpack-review-modular-adventure-camera-bag/01.avif" alt="mid" loading="lazy" />
<p>The padded back panel with breathable air mesh and the aluminum frame distribute weight effectively. Load lifter straps transfer weight from shoulders to hips — though taller users may find the torso length slightly short for optimal lift. The waist belt can be magnetically stowed for short walks and deployed for longer treks.</p>
<p>Shoulder strap pockets are large enough for an iPhone Max. A raised camera clip mount prevents digging into your shoulder.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Weather Protection</h2>
<p>Weather-resistant materials and a reinforced bottom handled rain, desert dust, and boat spray without issue during testing. The included rain cover adds an extra layer for downpours. The hidden AirTag pocket provides peace of mind during travel.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Limitations</h2>
<ul>
<li>Tripod carry could be improved — the side pocket works but isn&#039;t ideal for larger tripods.</li>
<li>Side access is only available on one side.</li>
<li>Accessory pockets on the top loader become difficult to reach when mounted inside the bag.</li>
<li>The white interior will likely show dirt over time.</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Practical Tips</h2>
<ul>
<li>Choose the top loader size based on your most common gear setup — Small for a body+lens, Large for a full trinity.</li>
<li>The removable tote is surprisingly useful for errands at your destination.</li>
<li>Use the accessory straps on the front panel for snowshoes, a tripod, or bulky layers.</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<p><strong>Q: Does the Shimoda Sidecountry have rear panel access?</strong> No — it&#039;s designed exclusively for side access. For rear access, consider the Shimoda Action X series.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Is it carry-on compatible?</strong> Yes — the 28L and 32L both meet most airline carry-on dimensions.</p>
<p><strong>Q: How does it compare to the Peak Design Outdoor Backpack?</strong> The Shimoda offers better hiking-specific ergonomics and modularity.
