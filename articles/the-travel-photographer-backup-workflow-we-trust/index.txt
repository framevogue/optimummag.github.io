10:T440,<p>The best backup system is boring, repeatable, and easy to verify when you are tired. Travel makes every weak link louder: small hotel desks, slow Wi-Fi, full cards, and early call times.</p>
<img src="/media/articles/terramaster-d1-ssd-enclosure-rugged-performance-and-pocket-friendly/01.avif" alt="Portable SSD enclosure on a dark surface" loading="lazy" />
<h2>Keep Three Copies</h2>
<p>Keep the card untouched until the job is delivered, copy the files to a fast SSD, and mirror that SSD to a second drive or cloud folder when the connection allows.</p>
<h2>Name Folders Before Import</h2>
<p>Use a simple structure: date, location, assignment, and camera. Clean folders make rushed editing less painful.</p>
<h2>Verify, Then Format</h2>
<p>Use software that can verify copies or compare checksums. A file that appears in Finder or Explorer is not the same as a file you have verified.</p>
<h2>Pack For Failure</h2>
<p>Bring two short cables, one long cable, a card wallet, and a small pouch that never leaves your personal bag.
