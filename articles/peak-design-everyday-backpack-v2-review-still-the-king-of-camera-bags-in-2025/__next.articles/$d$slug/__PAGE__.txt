3:Td4b,<p>The Peak Design Everyday Backpack has been the gold standard of camera bags since its 2015 Kickstarter debut. The V2 iteration refines the formula with improved shoulder straps, a stronger MagLatch system, and subtle quality-of-life upgrades that — for most users — justify the investment.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Key Specs (20L)</h2>
<ul>
<li>Capacity: 17L min / 20L max (expandable)</li>
<li>Weight: 2.01 kg / 4.43 lbs (with dividers)</li>
<li>Laptop: Up to 16&quot; MacBook Pro</li>
<li>Tablet: Up to 12.9&quot; iPad Pro</li>
<li>Material: 400D recycled nylon canvas, weatherproof</li>
<li>3 FlexFold dividers included</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>What&#039;s New in V2</h2>
<p><strong>Improved MagLatch:</strong> The magnetic top latch now holds more strongly — bags packed to maximum capacity no longer pop open unexpectedly.</p>
<p><strong>Redesigned shoulder straps:</strong> More ergonomic padding, better axial pivoting, and magnetic holders that keep straps tidy when stowing.</p>
<p><strong>Simplified interior pockets:</strong> Fewer but larger organizer pockets replace the V1&#039;s multi-compartment approach. Some users will miss the granular organization; others will appreciate the cleaner layout.</p>
<p><strong>Waist strap removed:</strong> Peak Design now sells this separately — a disappointment for photographers who relied on it for hiking.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Real-World Versatility</h2>
<img src="/media/articles/peak-design-everyday-backpack-v2-review-still-the-king-of-camera-bags-in-2025/01.avif" alt="mid" loading="lazy" />
<p>The FlexFold dividers remain the most intuitive camera bag organization system on the market. In one configuration, you can carry a DSLR with 24-70mm f/2.8, a flash, chargers, laptop, and tablet. Reconfigure in 30 seconds for a DJI Mavic drone plus accessories. Dump the dividers for a day pack. This is truly a one-bag solution.</p>
<p>Side access panels on both sides let you swing the bag around and grab your camera without setting it down — a feature that becomes essential once you&#039;ve used it.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Alternatives</h2>
<p><strong>Peak Design Everyday Zip:</strong> Simpler, lighter, cheaper. Loses the MagLatch and side access panels but keeps the FlexFold system.</p>
<p><strong>Nomatic McKinnon Camera Pack:</strong> Larger (35L), heavier, and tougher. Better for multi-day adventures but overkill for daily carry.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>If you don&#039;t already own a V1, the V2 is an easy recommendation — it&#039;s the most versatile, thoughtfully designed camera bag in its class. Existing V1 owners face a harder decision: the upgrades are genuine but incremental. For anyone seeking a bag that transitions seamlessly from office commute to weekend shoot to international travel, the Peak Design Everyday Backpack V2 remains the benchmark.</p>
<hr />
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<p><strong>Q: Does the 20L fit a 16&quot; laptop?</strong> Yes — the 20L model holds up to a 15/16&quot; MacBook Pro. The 30L model accommodates most 16&quot; laptops.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Is the V2 compatible with Peak Design Camera Cubes?</strong> Yes — Camera Cubes integrate with the FlexFold divider system for additional organization.</p>
<p><strong>Q: How weather-resistant is the Everyday Backpack?</strong> The 400D nylon canvas with DWR coating handles rain confidently.
