10:Tb47,<img src="/media/articles/sigma-24-70mm-f-2-8-dg-dn-ii-review-90-of-g-master-quality-at-half-the-price/01.avif" alt="Camera lens" loading="lazy" />
<p>The Sigma 24-70mm f/2.8 DG DN II makes a compelling argument against paying the Sony G-Master premium. Over several months of professional use — ski shoots, commercial work, landscapes, and gimbal video — this lens proved itself as a true workhorse that delivers roughly 90% of the flagship&#039;s performance for around half the cost.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Key Specs</h2>
<ul>
<li>Focal length: 24-70mm (full-frame Sony E)</li>
<li>Aperture: f/2.8 – f/22</li>
<li>Weight: 735g (1.6 lbs) — 100g lighter than V1</li>
<li>MFD: 6.7&quot; (17 cm)</li>
<li>Filter: 82mm</li>
<li>Weather-sealed throughout</li>
<li>De-clickable aperture ring, AF/MF switch, 2× custom focus buttons</li>
<li>No optical stabilization</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Build &amp; Handling</h2>
<p>The lens combines metal and high-quality plastic into a body that feels sturdy without being bulky. The 100g weight reduction from the original Sigma makes a real difference on gimbals — this version balances on a DJI RS3 Pro where the V1 didn&#039;t. The zoom and focus rings are well-damped and tactile. The aperture ring de-clicks for video work.</p>
<p>The barrel extends while zooming and there&#039;s no optical stabilization, but neither is a dealbreaker given modern Sony IBIS performance and the compact form factor tradeoff.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Autofocus &amp; Image Quality</h2>
<p>AF is fast, accurate, and fully compatible with Sony Eye AF and tracking. At a high-speed ski shoot with subjects moving 60+ mph, the lens never lost lock. It&#039;s virtually silent — a major plus for video.</p>
<p>At f/2.8, center sharpness is excellent, and edge performance holds up well. Stopping to f/4-5.6 delivers tack-sharp results across the frame. Bokeh is smooth for a zoom. Vignetting is present at f/2.8 but easily corrected. Chromatic aberration is well-controlled.</p>
<p>One caveat: third-party lenses are capped at 15 fps on Sony a1/a9 bodies — a limitation worth knowing for sports shooters.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Value &amp; Alternatives</h2>
<p>At under $1,200, this represents exceptional value. The Sony 24-70mm GM II is slightly sharper and lighter with no frame-rate limits but costs nearly double. The Tamron 28-75mm f/2.8 G2 is more affordable but sacrifices the 24mm wide end. The original Sigma V1 is heavier with fewer controls — buy it only if significantly discounted.</p>
<hr />
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<p><strong>Q: Is this lens weather-sealed?</strong> Yes — sealing at key points throughout the barrel.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Does it work well on a gimbal?</strong> Yes — it&#039;s compact and light enough for DJI RS3 Pro setups.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Is it sharp wide open?</strong> Very — especially in the center.
