3:Tabe,<img src="/media/articles/canon-eos-r5-mark-ii-review-the-hybrid-powerhouse-that-tempted-a-sony-shooter/01.avif" alt="Camera equipment at Triggar VR booth at SVVR 2015 (closeup)" loading="lazy" />
<p>As a Sony shooter for nearly a decade, I didn&#039;t expect the Canon R5 Mark II to tempt me toward a system switch — but after several weeks of intensive testing, it very nearly did. This camera raises the bar in stills resolution, autofocus intelligence, and video capability while remaining intuitive and responsive in fast-paced environments.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Key Specs</h2>
<ul>
<li>45MP full-frame stacked BSI CMOS sensor</li>
<li>Dual Pixel Intelligent AF with Eye Control</li>
<li>8K 60p RAW, 4K 120p 10-bit video</li>
<li>30 fps electronic shutter with Pre-Continuous Shooting</li>
<li>5.76M-dot EVF with OVF Simulation</li>
<li>In-camera upscaling to 179MP</li>
<li>Dual slots: CFexpress + SD UHS-II</li>
<li>Weight: 738g / 1.63 lbs</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Build &amp; Handling</h2>
<p>The magnesium alloy body with deep grip feels secure even with heavier lenses. The fully articulating touchscreen and tactile, well-placed dials make operation intuitive — I had the layout mastered within hours despite coming from Sony&#039;s ecosystem. The menu system is familiar and highly customizable.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Autofocus &amp; Low Light</h2>
<p>AF locks instantly with pixel-level precision in daylight and remains reliable in low light. Eye-tracking works seamlessly for people and animals. The touchscreen focus override is particularly useful for tracking subjects through obstructions.</p>
<p>Low-light performance is excellent: ISO 6400 files retain good detail, and with AI noise reduction in Lightroom, even ISO 12800 images are print-ready. Dynamic range is impressive, though it drops about a stop when using the electronic shutter.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Video &amp; Features</h2>
<p>8K 60p RAW and 4K 120p are serious tools. The optional cooling fan eliminates overheating concerns that plagued the original R5. Dual card slots, excellent IBIS, and speedy wireless transfer round out a pro-grade feature set.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Value &amp; Caveats</h2>
<p>At just under $4,000 body-only, it&#039;s an investment. The main concern is Canon&#039;s RF lens ecosystem: fewer affordable third-party options exist compared to Sony E-mount. Factor lens costs into your budget before committing.</p>
<hr />
<h2>FAQ</h2>
<p><strong>Q: Does the R5 II overheat during 8K recording?</strong> Heat management is significantly improved over the original R5, but an external cooling fan is recommended for extended 8K shoots.</p>
