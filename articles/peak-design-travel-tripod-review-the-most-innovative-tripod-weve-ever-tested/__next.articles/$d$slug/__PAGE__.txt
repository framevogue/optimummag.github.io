<p>Peak Design spent years secretly developing what may be their most ambitious product to date: a travel tripod redesigned entirely from the ground up. Instead of slapping their logo on an existing design — a common shortcut in the tripod market — they reimagined every component from scratch. The result is genuinely unlike anything else on the market.</p>
<hr />

<h2>Design &amp; Build Quality</h2>
<p>The defining feature of this tripod is its space-saving leg design — there is essentially no wasted air between the legs or the head when collapsed. The carbon fiber version looks stunning and feels significantly stiffer than its aluminum counterpart.</p>
<p>The build quality is exceptional. Materials and workmanship are superior to the generic knock-off brands that repackage the same basic tripod design. This is a product engineered from first principles.</p>
<h3>Leg Locks</h3>
<p>The lever-lock design has five leg sections per leg — meaning four locks per leg. These are some of the stiffest, strongest legs we&#039;ve ever encountered. The large hooked design makes them easy to operate, even in cold weather with gloves. An easy-access Allen key tightening screw is built in (the included key is hidden in a secret pocket on the tripod bag).</p>
<h3>Ball Head</h3>
<p>The ball head is the most ingenious part. The center column itself is part of the head — they&#039;re one integrated component. When completely collapsed, the clamp slips down between the leg angle platform, completing the &quot;zero empty space&quot; design. The Arca-Swiss clamp is strong enough to hold a full-frame DSLR with a flagship f/2.8 zoom.</p>
<p>One compromise: there&#039;s no dedicated panning knob, which may frustrate panorama photographers.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Real-World Performance</h2>
<p>The tripod produces remarkably sharp images at slow shutter speeds. Despite its compact size, it&#039;s among the stiffest travel tripods available — holding cameras steady even with heavier telephoto lenses. The ball head allows precise composition, though locking it down requires slightly more attention than a traditional design.</p>
<p>A clever hidden feature: a fold-out smartphone stand is concealed within the center column, secured magnetically when not in use.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Carbon Fiber vs Aluminum</h2>
<p>Carbon fiber isn&#039;t just about weight savings (2.81 lbs vs 3.44 lbs). It&#039;s significantly stiffer, which translates to sharper images with heavier gear, especially in light wind. Carbon fiber also resists the &quot;vibrato&quot; that plagues aluminum tripods with long lenses, and feels warmer to the touch in cold conditions.</p>
<p>For casual use with lightweight setups, the aluminum version at nearly half the price is an excellent choice. For demanding conditions or heavier glass, the carbon fiber model justifies its premium.</p>
<hr />
<h2>Practical Tips</h2>
<ul>
<li>Remove the safety pins on the Arca-Swiss clamp with the included Allen key if you use third-party plates.</li>
<li>For maximum stability, avoid extending the center column fully — keep it as low as possible.</li>
<li>Practice the one-handed deployment motion before heading into the field; the unique leg design rewards familiarity.</li>
<li>The tripod fits in most water bottle pockets and side sleeves — ideal for one-bag travel.</li>
</ul>
<hr />
<h2>Verdict</h2>
<p>Simply put, this is the best compact travel tripod on the market for photographers who value space efficiency above all else. No other tripod is this low-profile while remaining this strong and tall. While ultralight hikers may prefer options in the ~2 lb range, most photographers traveling with a single tripod will find this to be their best choice.</p>

<p><strong>Q: Can I use the tripod with non-Peak Design plates?</strong> Yes. The clamp accepts standard Arca-Swiss plates. Remove the safety pins with the included Allen key for full compatibility.</p>
<p><strong>Q: Is the smartphone holder built into the tripod?</strong> Yes — it&#039;s magnetically stored inside the center column. Twist the hook at the base to deploy it.</p>
